Existing Designs vs Our Improvements

Tokyo already uses advanced earthquake-resistant designs such as base isolation, flexible steel frames, and shock absorbers. These systems allow buildings to move safely during earthquakes. Our design improves on this by combining all systems together and increasing energy absorption, reducing damage even further.

How Earthquakes Form

Earthquakes occur when tectonic plates get stuck and pressure builds over time. When the pressure becomes too strong, the plates suddenly slip and release energy in the form of seismic waves, causing the ground to shake.

Why Tokyo is Earthquake-Prone

Tokyo is located on the Ring of Fire, where multiple tectonic plates meet. This makes earthquakes frequent and sometimes very powerful. The high population also increases the risk of damage and casualties.

Main Hazards (Short-Term & Long-Term)

Short-term: Ground shaking, collapsing buildings, fires, and injuries.

Long-term: Economic damage, rebuilding costs, homelessness, and mental stress.

Challenges After the Disaster

After an earthquake, people may struggle to find shelter, food, and clean water. Roads and infrastructure can be damaged, making rescue efforts difficult. Communication systems may fail, and recovery can take a long time.
